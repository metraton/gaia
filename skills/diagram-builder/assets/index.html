How to read this diagram

This diagram is meant to explain itself. Here is the whole visual vocabulary — what each shape, color and control means. Try the chips at the top of the page as you read.

1 · Component

The atom of the diagram: one box. It carries a small status badge, a title, and a short description. Click any box to open its full detail in the bottom bar.

2 · Status & variant

The status is the word on the badge (ENTRY, EXPOSED, WEAK, NEW, HARDENED…). The variant is the color/border that carries the meaning: red = risk, amber = weak, green = safe/new, dashed = external.

3 · Section (zone)

A dashed zone that groups related components under a title. Its own color flags the whole group — a red zone (danger) or a green zone (safe) qualifies everything inside it.

4 · Subsection

A section can split into labeled subsections, each its own little grid of components — a second level of grouping inside one zone.

5 · Envelope

A section that wraps other sections — a borderless dashed boundary drawn around them (e.g. a cloud environment holding several zones).

6 · Columns, rows & span

The canvas is a grid of columns. Each cell declares its row and how many columns it spans. Columns are authoritative: a wide diagram scrolls sideways rather than shrinking its cells.

7 · Chips = flows

The chips at the top are flows. Tap one to spotlight every component in that flow and dim the rest; the bottom bar then explains the flow step by step. Tap All to reset. Try it above.
