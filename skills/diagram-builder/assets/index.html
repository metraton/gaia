How to read this diagram

This diagram is meant to explain itself. Here is the whole visual vocabulary — what each shape, color and control means. Try the chips at the top of the page as you read.

1 · Component

The atom of the diagram: one box. It carries a small status badge, a title, and a short description. Click any box to open its full detail in the bottom bar.

2 · Status & variant

The status is the word on the badge (ENTRY, EXPOSED, WEAK, NEW, HARDENED…). The variant is the color/border that carries the meaning: red = risk, amber = weak, green = safe/new, dashed = external.

3 · Section

A dashed zone that groups related things under a title. Its own color flags the whole group — a red section (danger) or a green section (safe) qualifies everything inside it. A section is a grid: it can hold components directly, or nest other sections inside itself — the same shape, as deep as the idea needs.

4 · Nesting

Because a section's children can themselves be sections, a big group (e.g. a whole environment) can wrap smaller groups inside it — a recursive grid of grids, all the way down to the boxes at the leaves.

5 · Component types

A component (leaf) defaults to a box. Two other kinds exist for pure layout: a separator — a thin divider with no content — and a rail — a swimlane-style label banner. Both are structural, not data-carrying.

6 · Columns & merge

Every section is a grid of same-size cells. A child can declare span to merge across columns — an Excel-style merge — occupying more than one cell (a box can span N columns; span == the section's own column count makes it a full-width band). As the window gets narrower, columns cascade down — 3 → 2 → 1 — until the whole diagram is one stacked column. Cells never resize; only the column count does, so nothing ever needs to scroll sideways.

7 · Order & bands

Sections render in their authored order, packing side by side until a row fills up. A section that spans every column becomes a full-width band and always takes its own row — place a band last to pin it as a base layer at the very bottom of the page.

8 · Chips = flows

The chips at the top are flows. Tap one to spotlight every component in that flow and dim the rest; the bottom bar then explains the flow step by step. Tap All to reset. Try it above.
